A Great
Mango Salsa

Ripe mango, sharp red onion, lime, and a whisper of heat — five minutes of chopping for a bowl that brightens anything it touches.

Prep
10 min
Rest
15 min
Yield
3 cups
Serves
4–6
i.

Ingredients

  • Ripe mangoes, diced2 large
  • Red bell pepper, small dice½
  • Red onion, finely diced¼ cup
  • Jalapeño, seeded & minced1
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped⅓ cup
  • Lime juice, fresh2 tbsp
  • Flaky sea salt½ tsp
  • Honey, optional1 tsp
ii.

Method

  1. Soak the diced red onion in cold water for five minutes, then drain. This tames the bite without killing the crunch.
  2. Peel and dice the mangoes into roughly quarter-inch cubes — pieces that hold their shape on a chip.
  3. In a medium bowl, combine mango, bell pepper, drained onion, jalapeño, and cilantro. Toss gently with a rubber spatula.
  4. Squeeze in the lime juice and sprinkle the salt. If your mangoes lean tart, stir in a teaspoon of honey to balance.
  5. Rest fifteen minutes at room temperature so the flavors marry. Taste, adjust salt and lime, then serve.

A note on mangoes

The best variety for salsa is Ataulfo — small, golden, almost fiberless, honey-sweet. If yours are rock-hard, give them two days on the counter in a paper bag before cutting.

iii.

Pairs well with

  • grilled mahi-mahi
  • pork carnitas tacos
  • crispy tortilla chips
  • black bean tostadas
  • coconut rice bowls
  • a very cold beer
